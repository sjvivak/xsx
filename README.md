## Who Is XSX Network?

Founded in 2012 with roots in Linux and budget North American VPS hosting, XSX Network spent years under different brand names before settling into its current identity. For the past several years, their focus has been entirely on Asia — specifically Singapore, Hong Kong, and Japan. In 2025, they announced plans to re-enter the North American market, though at the time of writing those services are still in preparation.

Their infrastructure is solid on paper: host nodes run AMD EPYC processors with NVMe SSD storage in RAID10, and the company accepts both PayPal and Alipay, which is a practical touch for their Asian customer base.

## What They Actually Offer

XSX Network's product lineup is clean and simple — Asia KVM VPS across three node locations, plus dedicated servers in Singapore and Tokyo. There's no bloated feature list, no upsell maze. You pick your plan, you pick your location, and you're done.

- Both IPv4 and IPv6

## What to Know Before You Buy

XSX Network is a lean operation. There's no live chat, no pre-sales phone line. Support runs through a ticket system, and you'll need to be a registered customer to open one. The team is responsive to tickets submitted through the portal, but don't go in expecting instant hand-holding.

The upside of that lean structure is lower overhead — which presumably contributes to the pricing. If you're comfortable managing your own Linux server and just need reliable hardware in Asia at a fair price, XSX Network delivers exactly that.
